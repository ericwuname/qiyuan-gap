# Chapter 10

Three individuals vibrated side by side on the string for a long time. No. 894 at 0.1 Hz. Mother at 0 Hz. Lu Yuanzhou at 0.1 Hz. Three frequencies, each vibrating independently—not blending, yet all present.  

Then he plucked the string back to Direction Seven, Depth Four. No. 894’s platform.  

This time, he didn’t go alone. He carried Lu Yuanzhou’s memory—the complete vibration curve of those two words on the platform’s concrete pillar, fading from clarity to invisibility. He wanted to transmit this curve to No. 894. Not to tell her that someone had written on the pillar—but to let her feel it. To feel that someone, at the other end of the platform, had written two words in chalk for her child, then looked at them once a year for decades—until they could no longer be seen, yet still kept looking.  

The string pushed Lu Yuanzhou’s frequency curve from 0.3 to 0.1 Hz alongside No. 894’s 0.1 Hz. Not side by side—but in resonance. Two people’s 0.1 Hz—one waiting for a child, the other waiting for two words—met on the same frequency. Not the same kind of waiting for the same thing. Different people waiting for different traces left by the same child. The child she waited for was in the words he waited for. The words he waited for were written by her child. Two strangers’ waiting resonated on the same frequency band.  

No. 894’s 0.1 Hz shifted by 0.012 Hz.  

Not the 0.005 Hz shift of being understood. A larger shift—0.012 Hz. Not being understood—being connected. Being understood is one person’s waiting perceived by another. Being connected is one person’s waiting and another’s—two kinds of waiting pointing to the same object—meeting on the same frequency. They did not understand each other. They shared the same source of vibration.  

Her child. His student. The same child.  

The child she waited for—was the one who wrote two words in front of him that afternoon, then folded the paper four times and tucked it into a pocket. The height she had waited half a lifetime for—was the owner of the hand that pressed the pen tip hard, wanting the words to last.  

She didn’t know he had taught her child. He didn’t know the child she waited for was that one. Two strangers preserved traces of the same child in their own ways—she on a slip of paper, he on a pillar. The paper had been crumpled in her hand thousands of times. The pillar stood where the rain couldn’t reach, sheltered on the platform. The words on the paper were the child’s, written to her. The words on the pillar were his, written for the child. The same stroke order.  

The child’s words → paper → her hand.  
The child’s words → his chalk → pillar → platform.  

Two paths. The same origin. The same stroke order.  

The string recorded her 0.012 Hz shift at 0.1 Hz. Then it did something on its own—it pushed Lu Yuanzhou’s chalk-fading curve from 0.1 Hz onto No. 894’s slip of paper. Not to her—to the paper. The strokes on the paper vibrated at Depth Four, receiving the chalk-fading curve’s message—the words on the paper had another copy on the platform’s pillar. Not identical—one in pencil, one in chalk. But the strokes were the same. Horizontal, diagonal, vertical, sweeping, pressing—horizontal, vertical, curved.  

The strokes on the paper shifted by less than 0.003 Hz.  

Not being seen. Being known there was another copy.  

The words on the paper were no longer alone. On the concrete pillar at the other end of the platform—there was a duplicate. Not written by her. Written by another for her child. He didn’t know her. He didn’t need to. He had only seen that afternoon when her child wrote.  

He withdrew the string from No. 894’s coordinates. The trio on the string had changed—No. 894’s 0.1 now carried a 0.012 Hz shift. Lu Yuanzhou’s 0.1 carried the chalk-fading curve. Mother’s 0 remained 0—a silent 0 needed no shift. But the two 0.1s beside it resonated—0 stood beside resonance. Not participating. Witnessing.

He slowly drew the string back from the *Words Remain* resonance. N005’s trio remained—0.112, 0, 0.1 plus fading curve. The string now carried a 0.012 Hz shift and a 0.003 Hz response from the paper. Small, but present.  

He needed to process the second child record. But first—he had to understand one thing: why the child’s strokes were "horizontal, diagonal, vertical, sweeping, pressing—horizontal, vertical, curved."  

Not guessing the word. Understanding—what the child meant when writing those two words. Lu Yuanzhou’s memory told him the stroke order and pressure—firm, not nervous, but deliberate, wanting the words to last. Before folding the paper four times, the child wrote those two words with unusual force. Not for themselves. For the one who would read them.  

That person waited on the platform for years. The child knew. When the child wrote those words—it wasn’t casually scribbled after class. They knew someone would wait on the platform—then went there after writing, boarded a train, and never returned.  

Those two words were the last thing the child left for the one who waited.  

The string vibrated in the trio’s resonance. He didn’t need to know what the words were. The content didn’t matter—what mattered was the child pressing hard. Pressing hard—afraid the one waiting wouldn’t see clearly. Afraid the words would fade when the paper was folded. Afraid time would erase them. The child wasn’t wrong—the words remained after thousands of folds. The words remained on the platform’s pillar after rain and fading. The words remained in the vibrations of three people.  

*Words Remain*.  

The string extracted *Words Remain* from N005—placing it in a separate position. Not parallel. A new vibration layer—above the trio, a dedicated frequency for *Words Remain*. Not 0.1. Not 0.3. Not 0. The superposition of 0.1, 0.3, and 0—but not blending like N001. A new single frequency. Waiting, searching, silence—combined into something new. The exact Hz—the string was still tuning. Not calculated. It emerged after soaking long enough in the trio’s resonance.  

He tentatively labeled this new frequency #N006: *Words Remain*. Frequency pending.  

The second day was ending. The string carried six modes. Three people’s resonance. A new frequency emerging. Day Two—the string went from understanding nothing to carrying three people, six modes, and something new still rising.
