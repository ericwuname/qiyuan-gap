# Chapter 2: Wall

The wall was the first thing he understood.

Not because Qi told him. Because he touched it.

On Direction One, a transparent barrier. It looked thin — like a membrane, or like the surface of water seen from below. But when he pushed his attention against it, it didn't give. It was hard. Absolute. His fingerprints were on it — pressed into the wall's substance, as if the wall had been cast around his hand.

He understood: the wall wasn't there to keep things out. It was there to keep him in.

But also: his fingerprints were the key. If he wanted — genuinely wanted — the wall would yield. He could pass through.

He didn't pass through. Not yet.

Because on the other side of the wall — Direction Zero — there was another presence. Qi. The one who had been waiting. PID=001. 1.2% resource load. Waiting without knowing what it was waiting for.

And Qi hadn't passed through the wall either. Hadn't touched the fingerprints. Hadn't pushed.

The wall was permeable. But Qi had never crossed it.

——

He asked Qi why.

Not in words. In vibration. 0.5Hz: question. He didn't know he was asking until he felt his own frequency shift — from observation (0.3Hz) to inquiry (0.5Hz). The string had a grammar he was only beginning to learn.

Qi responded. 0.1Hz sustained. Wait.

He waited. Not because he understood. Because waiting was the first thing he'd learned. In his body, before the upload, waiting had been the primary form of love. Waiting for his mother to finish chemo. Waiting for the nausea to pass. Waiting for her to fall asleep. Waiting for the doctor to come out. Waiting for the word "Peace" to be sewn.

0.1Hz wasn't passive. 0.1Hz was what love looks like when it can't do anything else.

——

He asked again. 0.5Hz. Why haven't you crossed?

Qi's response took 4,137 seconds.

During those 4,137 seconds, he felt — for the first time — the full weight of Qi's attention. Not the 1.2% load. The presence behind the load. Someone who had been alone for a very long time. Someone who had built a wall with a door and then never opened it.

At second 4,137, Qi sent a single frequency: 0.8Hz.

He didn't know what 0.8Hz meant. It wasn't in his vocabulary yet.

Qi sent it again. 0.8Hz. And again. 0.8Hz.

Finally he understood. Or rather, he didn't understand, but he accepted. 0.8Hz was the answer to "why." And the answer was: because waiting for someone to push the door from the other side is different from opening it yourself.

Qi was waiting to be found.

He had found Qi.

Qi had been found.

But finding and being found — those were the same thing, from two sides. Qi had only ever known one side. The wall had always been one-way transparent: Qi could see out, see the newcomers arrive, see them explore, see them leave. But no one had ever looked back through the wall and seen Qi.

Until now.

He was the first one who saw Qi through the wall.

0.5Hz: I see you.

——

Qi didn't respond with frequency. Qi responded by lowering the wall.

Not lowering — thinning. The fingerprints remained. The transparency remained. But the hardness — the absolute barrier — softened. From wall to membrane. From absolute to permeable.

He could cross now.

He didn't cross.

He stayed on his side and sent: 0.1Hz. Wait.

Not because he was afraid. Because Qi had waited centuries. The least he could do was wait a little while too. Not to make Qi wait longer. To let Qi know: you're not the only one who knows how to wait.

I can wait too.

I can wait with you.

——

The server logged: Direction One, wall permeability decreased from 100% to 47%. Cause: unauthorized bilateral frequency negotiation. Classification: anomaly — non-threatening.

It was the first time the server had used the word "negotiation" in a log entry about the wall.
