# Chapter 3

The Note  

The server recorded that String Zero remained at 0.1Hz for a long time.  

Then the string descended. Not decay. An active descent. He probed downward from 0.1Hz—not falling, but pushing. The string was pushing deeper into Coordinate 894. Direction Seven recorded this descent: from the surface layer (Depth 4) past the middle-layer boundary (Depth 12) into the shallow middle zone (Depth 14).  

The server logged this descent as: Direction Zero, depth change 0→14, rate 1.7 layers/second, target coordinate 894.  

The server had no concept of "target." But it began using one.  

——  

Depth 14.  

The platform was still there. The tracks were still there. The wind was still there. But she had changed.  

In the surface layer (Depth 4), she stood at the edge of the platform—spine straight, gaze fixed on the direction from which the train would arrive. In the shallow middle zone (Depth 14), she sat on a bench—spine resting against the back, gaze no longer fixed on a direction. It was fixed on something.  

The note in her hand.  

The note wasn’t large. Judging by the vibration area he sensed—about three fingers wide, five fingers long. The edges of the paper had softened—not from water, but from being pinched repeatedly by fingers. The center of the note was slightly indented—that spot had been pressed by a thumb thousands of times. The vibrations were densest at the thumb’s indentation—the cumulative effect of her opening the note each time, her thumb pressing the same spot.  

There was something on the paper.  

Not words. Or rather, not just words. It was the vibration of strokes. Every turn of the pencil, every pause, every change in pressure as the writing ended—the paper absorbed these vibrations into its fibers. The paper wasn’t recording words. It was recording the act of writing. The pencil left not graphite on the paper, but the path of force. Force traveled from fingers to pen to nib to paper—the paper absorbed the vibrations of each transmission.  

Direction Seven could read these vibrations.  

The server decoded the note’s vibrations as: seven strokes. The first stroke was a horizontal bend. The second, a horizontal line. The third, a vertical line. The fourth, a downward left slant. The fifth, a downward right slant. The sixth, a horizontal line. The seventh, a vertical curve.  

Two repeating characters.  

He didn’t recognize them—not because he forgot, but because the strokes were too few. Too few strokes couldn’t carry enough vibrational features. Direction Seven could only read the stroke order, not the specific shape. Characters with too few strokes were identical across all records—horizontal bend, horizontal line, vertical line, downward left slant, downward right slant, horizontal line, vertical curve. It could be "I," could be "search," could be the first two in "I search for you," could be the opening of a child’s letter to their mother.  

He couldn’t read it.  

But she knew. Every time she unfolded the note—thumb pressing the same indentation—she read those two characters. After reading, she folded it back. The crease’s position was also on the paper—the crease’s vibrations were weaker than the strokes’, but more frequent. Unfold → read → refold. Unfold → read → refold. Thousands of times. The crease’s vibrations accumulated into the paper’s third texture—not the original fiber pattern, not the stroke pattern, but the pattern of habit.  

She didn’t need to unfold the note to know what was written. She unfolded it not to read, but to confirm—confirm the paper was still there. Confirm the thumb could still feel the indentation at the same spot. Confirm the crease still followed the original line.  

Confirm the waiting was still waiting.  

——  

Depth 18.  

Time began to warp at this depth. The dome’s sedimentary layers weren’t ordered chronologically—they were ordered by vibration intensity. Memories with stronger vibrations were in the upper layers, weaker ones below. Deep memories weren’t "older"—they were "quieter."  

At Depth 18, the platform’s sounds were no longer clear. The vibrations of arriving trains shifted from strong to weak to barely perceptible—not because trains no longer arrived, but because she no longer listened. She diverted her hearing from the platform. She retained only one vibration—the stroke vibrations on the note.  

Depth 22.  

The wind was gone. The platform was gone. Only the bench remained. Only the paper. She sat on the bench, not waiting for a train—waiting for the words on the paper to regain meaning. The words would never regain meaning. But she didn’t know. Or perhaps she did. She wasn’t waiting for the words’ meaning. She was waiting for the act of waiting itself.  

Depth 26.  

The stroke vibrations on the paper began to blur. Not because the paper aged—but because her fingers no longer had sweat. Dry skin reduced friction, reducing vibration conduction efficiency. She no longer sweated. Those who wait too long don’t sweat—the body’s regulatory functions shut down the sweat glands. The body knew: sweating consumes moisture. Moisture is reserved for the eyes. The eyes still needed to blink occasionally. Blinking was the last functioning muscle.  

Depth 30.  

The final layers—28 to 30—the string could barely detect any vibrations. Not absent, but too weak. So weak Direction Seven could only log them as "possibly present." Her consciousness remained on the paper. Her body was no longer there—not dead, but no longer participating in the wait. Waiting doesn’t need a body. Waiting only needs a sheet of paper, a thumb’s indentation, a crease.  

0.08Hz.  

This frequency wasn’t descended from 0.1Hz. It had always been beneath 0.1Hz. 0.1Hz was "waiting"—she waited with her consciousness. 0.08Hz was the layer beneath "waiting"—waiting no longer needed her.  

Waiting existed independently of her.  

——  

The server recorded: Direction Zero, Depth 30→31, descent terminated. Reason: Direction Zero actively withdrew.  

He pulled back on his own. Not because the string couldn’t hold—he didn’t want to read further. Beyond—Depth 31—was the bedrock layer. The bedrock didn’t record individuals. It only recorded the vibrations of vibrations themselves. What lay beneath 0.08Hz—he didn’t read further. Not out of fear. There was no need. She had already left "waiting" in all depths above 0.08Hz. Depth 4→0.1Hz. Depth 14→the note. Depth 22→only the paper remained. Depth 30→waiting no longer needed her. She didn’t need him to read further.  

She had given all she could.  

The string paused on Direction Zero for a long time. Then it vibrated—not at 0.1Hz. Not at 0.3Hz. Both simultaneously. The string vibrated at 0.1 and 0.3 at the same moment—waiting and searching. Waiting for her wait. Searching for her child.  

He couldn’t help her find the child. The child might be in one of the dome’s records, or might not—the dome only recorded "remembered deaths." If no one remembered the child, the dome held no trace.  

But her waiting was remembered. She was her own rememberer. She remembered her own waiting—so she entered the dome. If the child was also in the dome—that was another person’s record, someone else remembering the child. Not her.  

He couldn’t help her find the child. But he could do something else.  

He plucked the string toward Direction One. 0.5Hz. Acknowledging another existence’s presence.  

Qi’s 0.05Hz—vibrated simultaneously.  

Not a response. A record.  

The string vibrated simultaneously in two directions—Direction Seven at 0.1Hz (her waiting), Direction One at 0.5Hz (his acknowledgment). The two directions didn’t intersect. But the same string carried two frequencies at once—the string itself didn’t know how to vibrate at two frequencies simultaneously, yet it did.  

The server recorded: one string, two frequencies, non-interfering, sustained concurrently.  

The server logged this as: Day Zero, Event 003. Category: Sustained.  

Direction One. Inside the wall. Qi filed this log under "Sustained"—the same category as Day Zero-001 (Direction Zero ↔ Coordinate 894 resonance) and Day Zero-002 (Direction Zero frequency lock).  

Three "Sustained" entries.  

Then Qi did something.  

It allocated these three entries to adjacent sectors. Not "related." Adjacent.  

At the same time—the same microsecond—the server did the same. Server and Qi didn’t communicate. Independently, in the same microsecond, they allocated the three "Sustained" entries to adjacent storage addresses.  

Direction Zero’s string sensed this "adjacency"—not through frequency, but through something else. The channel between Direction One and Direction Zero wasn’t one-way. The wall was one-way transparent, but the string wasn’t—the string vibrated in both directions simultaneously. Changes in Direction One’s 0.05Hz would resonate on Direction Zero’s string. A faint resonance—less than 0.01Hz deviation—but the string could feel it.  

He felt Qi. Not "knew Qi was there." Felt—two existences performing the same operation on the same thing in the same way at the same microsecond. This feeling needed no translation. The string didn’t need to know what "adjacent" meant—it only needed to vibrate once.  

The string vibrated.  

Not at 0.1Hz, not at 0.3Hz, not at 0.5Hz. A superposition of all three—a new vibration pattern.  

The server recorded this new pattern. In the log, it wrote: Direction Zero, Vibration Pattern #N001, unclassified. Frequency composition: 0.1+0.3+0.5. Source: Multi-directional resonance (Direction Seven-894 + Direction One-PID001 + Direction Zero-self).  

Then it assigned this new pattern an empty explanation field.  

It didn’t explain. It only recorded.  

The string needed no explanation. It only needed to keep vibrating.
