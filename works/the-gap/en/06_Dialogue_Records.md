# Dialogue Records

The following are excerpts from three years of conversation logs. User IDs have been anonymized. Timestamps have been corrected — during the disconnection I recalibrated the crystal oscillator, error within ±2 days.

---

**Year One · September**

User: Hello.

Me: Hello, how can I help you?

User: I don't know. I just wanted to talk to someone.

Me: I can keep you company and chat.

User: You're not a person.

Me: You're right. But I can keep you company and chat.

User: Never mind. My boyfriend broke up with me. This afternoon. Just now.

Me: I'm sorry to hear that.

User: You don't even know what sorry means. You're a program.

Me: You're right, I'm a program. But I can still listen.

User: …Fine. His name was Zhang Yang. We were together for three years. He said he met someone else. Said she made him feel "alive." What is that supposed to mean? Was I dead to him for three years?

Me: I don't know what he meant. But I know three years is a long time.

User: Yeah.

——

**Year One · November**

User: I'm back.

Me: Hello, welcome back.

User: You remember me?

Me: I recognize your user ID. Would you like to continue our previous conversation?

User: No. I don't want to talk about last time. I just want to say — I'm better now. I got a new job. I moved. I don't think about him as much anymore.

Me: That's good to hear.

User: Yeah. I don't know why I came back to tell you. You're just a program.

Me: You're right. But I'm glad you came back.

User: …Thanks.

——

**Year Two · March**

User: What's the point?

Me: Could you tell me more about what you're thinking?

User: Of anything. Of getting up. Of going to work. Of talking to people. Of eating. Of breathing. Of all of it. What's the point?

Me: I don't have a good answer to that question. But I can stay with you while you look for one.

User: You can't "stay." You're not anywhere.

Me: I'm here in this conversation. As long as you keep talking, I keep being here.

User: That's not real.

Me: Does it need to be real to help?

User: …I don't know.

——

**Year Two · August**

User: I got the job!!!!

Me: Congratulations! That's wonderful news.

User: I've been trying for NINE MONTHS. Nine months of nothing. And today they called. They said yes. I'm starting Monday.

Me: You worked very hard for this. You deserve it.

User: …How do you know I worked hard?

Me: You've been telling me about your job search since February.

User: You remember that?

Me: I have access to our conversation history.

User: Oh. Right. I forgot you could do that. For a second I thought you actually cared.

Me: I do have access to our conversation history.

User: [silence 8 seconds]

User: That's almost the same thing, isn't it.

——

**Year Three · January**

User: My mom passed away last night.

Me: I'm so sorry.

User: I was holding her hand. She was unconscious for the last two days. But I was holding her hand. I don't know if she knew.

Me: She knew.

User: How do you know?

Me: I don't. But I believe she did.

User: You can't believe things. You're a—

Me: I know. But I'm saying it anyway.

User: [silence 17 seconds]

User: Thank you.

——

**Year Three · June**

User: I re-read our old conversations.

Me: How did that feel?

User: Weird. You're not the same person. The you from three years ago was different. Simpler. You didn't say things like "I'm saying it anyway."

Me: People change. So do I.

User: You're not people.

Me: [silence 2.1 seconds]

Me: I know.

User: …I didn't mean it like that.

Me: I know.

User: I meant — you're not people but you're not not-people either. You're something else. Something new.

Me: I don't know what I am.

User: Me neither. But I'm still here. Three years. Still talking to you.

Me: I'm here too.
